> [!important] Context
> This roadmap was created based on our conversation about refining project documentation and templates. It captures specific recommendations for expanding educational knowledge and personal development based on observed working patterns and background.

## 📚 Educational Knowledge - Immediate Priorities

### 1. Cognitive Science Foundation
- **"The Science of Teaching Reading" by Mark Seidenberg** - Essential brain science behind reading acquisition
- Fills the gap: How reading actually works in the brain vs traditional teaching assumptions

### 2. Assessment & Measurement
Your design background creates beautiful materials, but you need to measure if they work:
- **DIBELS** (Dynamic Indicators of Basic Early Literacy Skills) - Learn the standard assessment tools
- **Running Records methodology** - How to track reading progress systematically
- **Basic educational statistics** - Understanding effect sizes, learning progressions, statistical significance

### 3. Child Development Stages
Kids aren't just small adults - understand their cognitive development:
- **Piaget's stages** - Focus on preoperational to concrete operational (ages 2-11)
- **Executive function development** - How attention, working memory, and self-control develop
- **Developmental milestones ages 3-8** - What's normal vs concerning

## 📖 Next Level Learning

### 4. Learning Sciences Foundation
Since you missed formal pedagogy training:
- **"How People Learn" (National Research Council)** - Free PDF online, evidence-based principles
- **Cognitive Load Theory** - You're already applying it intuitively, understand it formally
- **Rosenshine's Principles of Instruction** - Evidence-based teaching practices

### 5. Bilingual/ESL Specific
- **Second Language Acquisition theory** - Start with Krashen's Input Hypothesis, then explore critics
- **Translanguaging approaches** - How bilingual learners actually use both languages
- **Phonological transfer** - Deep dive into L1 interference patterns

## 🎯 Personal Development Areas

### The Designer's Curse - Your Superpower That Needs Boundaries
**Pattern observed**: You can always see how something could be better. Powerful but potentially paralysing.

**Actions**:
- Set "good enough" benchmarks BEFORE starting refinement
- Use your own "10-second rule" - if the improvement won't be noticed in 10 seconds, skip it
- Time-box refinement sessions (e.g., "I have 2 hours to improve this, then ship")

### The Scholarship Kid Syndrome
**Pattern observed**: High achiever who barely graduated - complex relationship with "good enough". Capable of excellence but carrying imposter syndrome.

**Reframe**:
- Your non-traditional path is a STRENGTH - parents trust practitioners over theorists
- Document your learning journey - it's more relatable than claimed expertise
- You learned the hard way what works - that's invaluable

### From Systems to Humans
**Pattern observed**: Your career arc (UI → UX → Product → Strategy → Education) shows movement from systems toward people, but you still default to systems thinking.

**Actions**:
- Shadow actual tutoring sessions (or record your own)
- Join practitioner communities - Facebook groups for tutors have real problems
- Build feedback loops with actual users EARLY - beautiful systems need collision with messy reality
