## EB Teaching Guide for Phonics and Reading to Bilingual Children in HK
[Link to doc]
Date conducted: 06/08/25

(Brief overview of doc, no more than 2-3 sentences)

> [!done] What this research establishes:
> ✅ Clear evidence for synthetic phonics methodology
   ✅ General progression sequence (SATPIN → phases)
   ✅ Understanding of Cantonese-specific challenges
   ✅ Best practices for flashcard design and use

> [!missing] What was missing for: practical implementation
>  **Exact word lists by phonics pattern** - We know the sequence but not the specific words to include at each level
**Validated sight word progression for ESL learners** - We have general targets but not the specific words in order
**Decodability percentages** - How to determine if a book matches a phonics level
**Cross-referencing with ORT levels** - Specific mapping between phonics phases and ORT stages


#### What was recommended to fill these gaps?
##### <mark class="hltr-orange">🔬 Further research</mark>
 
- **Specific word lists from established programs** (like Jolly Phonics, Letters and Sounds, or Floppy's Phonics) to ensure we're using validated combinations
- **High-frequency word lists specifically sequenced for ESL/EFL learners** rather than native speakers
- **Criteria for matching books to phonics levels** - what percentage of words should be decodable at each stage?
- **Cantonese-English specific word selection** - are there validated lists that account for L1 interference?
