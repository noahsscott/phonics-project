# SATPIN Sentence Progression (Group 1.1)

> [!resource] Resource Overview
> | **Resource ID**    | SP-001                                     |
> |--------------------|-------------------------------------------|

> | **Total Sentences**| 40+ sentences across 4 weeks              |
> | **Decodability**   | 100% SATPIN-only (no sight words needed)  |

## 📖 Week 1: VC Foundation (at, it, in, is, as, an)

### Day 1-2: Two-word sentences
- It is.
- At it.
- In it.
- As is.

### Day 3-4: Three-word sentences
- It is in.
- An ant is.
- At an inn.
- Is it in?

### Day 5: Four-word sentences
- It is at an inn.
- Is it in an inn?
- As it is in.

**Assessment sentence**: "It is in an inn."

---

## 📖 Week 2: CVC Core (sat, sit, pin, pan, tin, tap, nap)

### Day 1-2: Subject-verb basics
- Pat sat.
- Nan naps.
- Pat taps.
- Ann sits.

### Day 3-4: Adding objects
- Pat sat in it.
- Tap a pan.
- A pin in a tin.
- Nan sits at it.

### Day 5: Complex sentences
- Pat taps a tin pan.
- A pin is in a pan.
- Nan naps in a pit.
- Sit in a tan pit.

**Assessment sentences**:
- "Pat sat in a pan."
- "A tin pin is in it."

---

## 📖 Week 3: CVC Extended (tan, pat, pit, tip, sap, nit, sin, sip)

### Day 1-2: Action sentences
- Pat sips it.
- Tip a pan.
- A nit sits.
- Tap at a pit.

### Day 3-4: Descriptive sentences
- A tan pan tips.
- Pat is in a pit.
- Sip sap in a tin.
- A nit is in a tan pan.

### Day 5: Story sentences
- Pat sits in a tan pit.
- A nit sips sap.
- Tip it in a tin pan.
- Pat taps at a nit in a pit.

**Assessment sentences**:
- "A tan nit sits in a pit."
- "Pat sips sap in a tin pan."

---

## 📖 Week 4: Blends & Plurals (ant, apt, pant, past + plurals)

### Day 1-2: Final blends
- An ant pants.
- Pat is apt.
- Ants sit in tins.
- It is past a pan.

### Day 3-4: Initial blends (snap, spin, spit, span)
- Ants snap at it.
- Pat spins a pan.
- Spit in a pit.
- Span a tin.

### Day 5: Complex with plurals
- Ants snap at pins in tins.
- Pat spins pans past naps.
- Tins tip as ants pant.
- Pins snap in pits.

**Assessment sentences**:
- "Ants pant past pins in tins."
- "Pat snaps at nits in pans."

## 🎯 Teaching Tips for Sentences

### Progression Principles
1. **Start with 2-word sentences** - Build confidence
2. **Add one word at a time** - Gradual complexity
3. **Repeat key words** - Reinforcement through context
4. **Questions and statements** - Vary sentence types

### Scaffolding Strategies
| Challenge | Support Strategy |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Can't blend smoothly | Teacher reads first, student echoes |
| Loses place | Point to each word, use finger tracking |
| Forgets earlier words | Reread from beginning each time |
| No comprehension | Act out sentences with gestures |

### Error Correction Protocol
1. **Student reads**: "Pat sit" (missing -s)
2. **Teacher prompts**: "Let's check the ending. What letter is at the end?"
3. **Student self-corrects**: "Pat sits"
4. **Reread entire sentence**: For fluency

## 📊 Sentence Complexity Analysis

| Week | Words per Sentence | New Patterns | Cognitive Load |
|------|-------------------|--------------|----------------|
| 1 | 2-4 words | VC only | Low - word recognition |
| 2 | 2-5 words | CVC subjects/verbs | Medium - grammar emerges |
| 3 | 3-6 words | More verbs, adjectives | Medium-high - meaning focus |
| 4 | 4-7 words | Blends, plurals | High - morphology added |

## 💡 Differentiation Options

### Support Level
- Read only underlined words: "Pat **sat** in **it**."
- Complete the sentence: "Pat sat in a ___."
- Match sentence to picture
- Echo reading with teacher

### Core Level
- Read all sentences independently
- Answer yes/no questions: "Is Pat in a pan?"
- Draw what the sentence says

### Extension Level
- Create own SATPIN sentences
- Change one word: "Pat sat" → "Nan sat"
- Combine sentences: "Pat sat. + Pat naps." → "Pat sat and naps."
- Write sentences from dictation

## 🎌 Cantonese L1 Considerations

### Common Sentence-Level Errors
1. **Dropping "is"**: "It in" instead of "It is in"
   - Practice: Emphasise linking verb
   
2. **Final -s omission**: "Pat sit" instead of "Pat sits"
   - Practice: Tap for each word ending
   
3. **Article confusion**: "In tin" instead of "In a tin"
   - Practice: Count words before reading

### Cultural Adaptations
- Use familiar names: Nan, Ann (similar to Cantonese names)
- Avoid Western-specific contexts initially
- Include action verbs that translate clearly

## 📋 Quick Assessment Rubric

| Skill | Mastery = 90% | Developing = 70% | Needs Support < 70% |
|-------|---------------|------------------|---------------------|
| **Accuracy** | 9/10 words correct | 7/10 words correct | Fewer than 7/10 |
| **Fluency** | Natural phrasing | Word-by-word | Sound-by-sound |
| **Comprehension** | Can illustrate meaning | Understands with help | No understanding |
| **Expression** | Reads with meaning | Monotone but accurate | Struggles throughout |
